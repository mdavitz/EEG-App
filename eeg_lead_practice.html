10-20 System EEG Electrode Placement

In this practice module, you'll learn to place each electrode in its correct position.

Understanding the Interface

On the left side, you'll see a list of EEG leads. On the right side is a model of a human head where you'll place these leads.

Drag and drop the leads from the left panel onto the head model to place them.

The goal is to place each lead in its correct position according to the 10-20 system.

Naming Convention

EEG leads are named based on their location on the head:

  • Fp - Frontopolar (forehead)
  • F - Frontal
  • C - Central
  • P - Parietal
  • O - Occipital (back of head)
  • T - Temporal (sides of head)

Odd numbers (1, 3, 5, 7) indicate the left side of the head, while even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8) indicate the right side. The letter 'z' indicates the midline.

Basic Lead Positions

Here are the key positions to remember:

  • Fz - Front center
  • Cz - Top center (vertex)
  • Pz - Back center
  • Fp1, Fp2 - Forehead (left and right)
  • O1, O2 - Back of head (left and right)

Try placing these leads first as reference points.

Placing Leads

To place a lead:

  1. Click and hold on a lead from the left panel
  2. Drag it to the correct position on the head model
  3. Release to place it

If you're using a touch device, tap on a lead and then tap where you want to place it.

To remove a lead, drag it off the head model.

Checking Your Work

When you've placed all the leads, click the Check Placement button to see how accurately you've positioned them.

Leads will be highlighted in green if they're correctly placed, or red if they need adjustment.

You can also use the Reset All button to start over.

Montages

A montage refers to the arrangement of EEG channels during recording or display.

You can select different montages from the dropdown menu to see how leads are connected in clinical practice:

  • 10-20 System - Shows individual lead placements
  • Longitudinal Bipolar - Shows connections from front to back
  • Transverse Bipolar - Shows connections from left to right
  • Referential - Shows connections to a reference electrode
  • Laplacian - Shows connections to averaged surrounding electrodes
